African HDI Dashboard

This analysis of Human Development Index highlights a few African countries, Nigeria was the main focus for this projects.

There are four categories of development after I filtered out the African countries, so I took randomly two countries from each category except the category in which Nigeria fell on, I picked three (i.e Nigeria and two other countries). The HDI rank indicates how good a country is performing in terms of development. Nigeria falls on low end of medium human development category.

The three main factors the determines the Human Development Index (HDI) are; 1. Life expectancy at birth 2. Education level(Mean and Expected years of schooling) 3. GNI per capita (Gross National Income per capita)

The difference between our GNI per capita and that of the leading country is very wide and that is one of the reasons why we have a low HDI. The GNI per capita rank minus HDI rank takes the forms of positive and negative. If the result is negative, it means that the GNI rank is higher than the HDI rank which means the standard of living is high and there's low performance in other areas of development If the result is positive, it means GNI rank is lower than the HDI rank which means the country's economic standing is lower than its overall human development and if the result is neutral, it means the GNI is similar to HDI rank indicating a balanced level of development.

Soft Drink Dashboard

The dataset contains information on soft drink distribution in Alimosho Local Government Area, Lagos State, Nigeria. During the data cleaning process, duplicate entries were removed, and cells containing multiple products were separated into individual rows to ensure each product had a distinct entry in the product column. For entries labeled as "Other," the corresponding values from the "Other" column were used. Column headers were renamed for clarity, and product names and shelf presence columns were standardized for consistency.

Key Findings from the Analysis:

1. Distribution by Product: Coca-Cola had the largest distribution in the area, followed closely by Pepsi and Fanta. Products such as Super Commando, Sosa, Smoov, Pop Apple, Horse Power, Freshyo, and Cway were the least distributed according to the survey.

2. Distribution by Outlet Type: Shops recorded the highest distribution of soft drinks, followed by kiosks, though the gap between them was substantial. Restaurants, bars, and supermarkets had the lowest distribution in this region of Lagos.

3. Storage Levels per Outlet: Most outlets had an evenly distributed stock of soft drinks. In some outlets, Coca-Cola was the dominant product, while for others, no single product clearly dominated.

4. Stock Conditions: Coca-Cola, Fanta, and Pepsi were generally well-stocked across most outlets, though the order of dominance varied by outlet. In some outlets, American Cola or Bigi also had significant stock levels. A few outlets had less than half of the products listed in the survey in their inventory.

Insights and Practical Implications:

Consumers can now have a clear expectation of product availability in Alimosho, helping them plan their purchases without surprises.

The dataset includes geographic coordinates, allowing individuals to identify which outlets carry specific products and navigate directly to them.

Arsenal All-Time PL Dashboard

This dashboard involves web scraping to get an all time stats of Arsenal Football club against different premier league clubs as at 6th November 2025.

The data contains the total games played against each team selected, total number of goals scored, total number of goals conceded, total games won, drawn or lost.

The dashboard basically just gives a detailed summary of the team of interest. I used Arsenal because that is the team I support and I thought it would be fun to just do something that would make it easy to draw up stats with the proper visual.
